Hi! I’m Sunny.

I'm an astrophysicist working at the Nicholas and Lee Begovich Center for Gravitational-Wave Physics and Astronomy. My research work focuses on gravitational-wave inference, dense-matter nuclear physics, and statistical analysis of complex, multi-faceted data sets. Now I'm looking to bring that same rigor to the data science and machine learning space.

Research

Gravitational waves, nuclear astrophysics, and thoughtful statistics.

Gravitational waves

Ripples in the fabric of spacetime, probed with waveform modeling, parameter estimation, and Bayesian inference using gravitational-wave data.

Nuclear astrophysics

Dense matter in neutron star cores connects microphysics to macroscopic observables, helping us learn more about the fundamental nature of matter at extreme densities.

Statistical analysis

Rigorous treatment of astronomical events and their data: cross-validation, systematic checks, and honest uncertainty where discovery depends on the data.

Neutron star Equation of State Inference

Sunny Ng et al. 2025 Class. Quantum Grav. 42 205008

Inferring the neutron star equation of state with nuclear-physics informed semiparametric models

My first-author published paper on semiparametric modeling of the neutron star equation of state. Semiparametric modeling connects nuclear-physics priors to astrophysical constraints with robust correlation modeling: Bayesian inference on the dense-matter equation of state with flexibility where the microphysics is uncertain.
